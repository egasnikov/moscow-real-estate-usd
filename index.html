In ruble terms Moscow housing kept rising, but priced in dollars it nearly halved after the 2014 devaluation and has only partly recovered as the ruble strengthened back toward 77. The currency move, not the local market, drove the dollar price. Offices have outrun housing since 2022 on a squeeze in quality supply.

Sources: residential = IRN.RU Moscow secondary index (ex-elite); USD/RUB = annual weighted averages, audit-it.ru / CBR; office line indicative, modelled from Knight Frank / NF Group market commentary. Dollar prices = ruble price ÷ annual average USD/RUB.
